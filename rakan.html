Project Rakan

Group project

A SaaS for voters, lawyers, and politicians to analyze gerrymandering.

C++, CMake, Docker, Google Test

Role Co-founder + Senior Engineer
Date March 2020 — June 2020
Link GitHub + documentation

Goals

Gerrymandering is an unfair political strategy used to manipulate district boundaries to guarantee a winning party within a state. See a quick example.

Project Rakan seeks to challenge gerrymandering by analyzing the likelihood of a map being gerrymandered and providing visualization to real-time redistrictings, details in this extensive documentation.

What I Did

I managed the team of six developers in an Agile/Scrum setting along with my co-founder. Specifically, I reviewed the team's agenda, goals, and challenges during the weekly sprint meetings. I assisted in designing and maintaining a RESTful API contract between the front-end and the back-end.

I also led the back-end team in developing C++ code, maintaining CI/CD and code reviews on GitHub, and assuring product quality via unit tests using the Google Test framework.

In developing C++ code, I efficiently and correctly managed 13 data structures to optimized the look-up, insertion, and deletion time of frequently accessed data. I also implemented various algorithms such as BFS and Metropolis-Hastings.

What I Learned

First and foremost, I learned to adapt to and appreciate test-driven development. In my reflection, I mentioned the back-end bug that caused the beta-release of Rakan to be a failure. I took responsibility for the oversight and strived to fix the bug, which I was able to do so via extensive testing. This made me believe in test-driven development and test-driven debugging.

Secondly, I learn to communicate and collaborate with a large team efficiently. As the co-founder, I assisted in helping the front-end and back-end communicate both in-person and on code through a RESTful API. I also learned to collaborate through pair programming which the back-end team to both ensure my teammate is up to pace and help me think through my code.
